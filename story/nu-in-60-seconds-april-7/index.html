NU in 60 Seconds: April 7
By Aubrey Blanche
10:08 p.m. April 6 2008

    Sex Week officially kicks off today with the theme of “Health, Pleasure, and Butterflies.” Tons of events are going on in celebration of carnality. Tomorrow are some fun events for people of all tastes:

    Sexual Health & Assault Resource Fair
    12:30 to 3:30 p.m., Norris Wildcat Rooms
    Premiere Screening of “Mekong Butterflies” and Expert Sex Trafficking Panel
    7 p.m., Norris McCormick Auditorium
    Rock Your/Her/His World Tonight: The ins and outs of great solo and partner sex
    9 p.m., Tech LR3

    The Norris Box Office promises to be busy, as tickets go on sale at 10:30 a.m for the “interactive conversation” with SNL stud Andy Samberg. If you needed a weekend to think about whether you want to see the Counting Crows in concert, tickets are still available for that too.

    What to know:

    Now that the mandatory info session has passed, you can’t run for ASG president. But five people are, and campaign season has officially begun. Mark Crain appears to have already gained some prime chalk-on-the-ground real estate with his colorful flyers.

    In case you were at the Sigma Chi pledge party this weekend, Graffiti dancers were “Off the Wall” at the McCormick auditorium in Norris. The group, which bills itself as “NU’s most eclectic dance ensemble,” had their spring show Thursday, Friday and Saturday night.
