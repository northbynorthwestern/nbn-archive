Physics made fun
By Ryan Gallagher
3:36 a.m. Nov. 14 2007

    When I think back upon my experience with physics, I think of the damn four I received on my AP Physics exam. No, not because it’s a bad score, but because Northwestern only accepts fives in physics for credit. My school’s valedictorian didn’t even get a five! So much for working my ass off .

    Luckily, Kloonigames has made a great physics game for the rest of us. Crayon Physics Deluxe is tablet-PC based game in which players draw objects in order get pre-positioned balls to touch a small star. Sound boring? Well, I’ll just let the video do the justice for me.

    Looks fun, no? The best part is the complete creative freedom you have to draw anything you want within the game’s environment. It kind of reminds me of the demo we saw of Little Big Planet, a PS3 game now scheduled for release in 2008.

    Too bad I don’t have a tablet PC. Who knows, perhaps we’ll see an iPhone port of this game on Install.app soon. Pretty please?
