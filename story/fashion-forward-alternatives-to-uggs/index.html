Fashion forward alternatives to Uggs
By Tegan Reyes
9:55 a.m. Jan. 25 2011

    It’s happened to everyone. That feeling of instant panic, arms flailing, scraped palms–you curse the unsalted, icy sidewalk. If only you had the right footwear. Here are five stylish, practical alternatives to Uggs.

    Sorel: Women’s 1964 Premium
    Price: $110.00
    Color: Deep teal, cinder, black

    Photo courtesy of Sorel.

    The stylish patent leather exterior is waterproof allowing for comfortable and dry feet in even the wettest conditions. Designed to keep feet warm down to 40 degrees below zero, the interior is a thick removable felt liner. The rubber shell with herringbone outsole provides great traction to boot.

    Eddie Bauer Mountain Pac Boots
    Price: $125.00
    Color: Tan

    Photo courtesy of Eddie Bauer.

    These traditional men’s boots are durable and warm and are weather rated to 25 degrees below zero. The top of the boots are made of leather and have rubber bottoms with a rubber outsole providing for great traction. The removable wool liners add to the warmth of the boot and extra liners can be purchased if needed. The traditional style and neutral color make it a simple boot that can be worn with anything.

    Photo courtesy of The North Face.

    The North Face Women’s Nuptse Bootie III
    Price: $85.00
    Color: White, black, brown

    The neutral colors and simple design will go great with any outfit, and the easy slip-on fit is great for when you are running late to class. The insulated bootie promises to keep you warm in the coldest of temperatures. The Ice Pick temperature- sensitive rubber pads on the sole are meant to stiffen when the temperature drops providing for a secure grip all throughout the day.

    Photo courtesy of Bogs.

    Bogs Classic High Handles
    Price: $92.00
    Colors: Black

    Bogs footwear, available for both men and women, provides comfortable, warm boots with great traction. The handles and flexibility of the boot make for an easy slip-on fit but stay snugly against the leg for warmth. The layers of waterproof rubber keep your feet dry and cozy, and the tread of the non-slip sole provides excellent traction for those long icy walks.

    Photo courtesy of Teva.

    Women’s Teva Kiru Boot
    Price: $140.00
    Colors: White, black and brown.

    Although these boots are expensive, they’ll keep you comfortable and free of embarrassing falls. The neutral colors will match anything in your wardrobe, and they don’t have the bulky look of other snow boots. The lace up look and faux fur will keep you looking chic and your feet warm as you walk down Sheridan.

    Investing in a durable, warm pair of winter boots is a necessity for Northwestern students. If none of the above are suitable, look into the boots from L.L. Bean, Columbia or Lands’ End.
