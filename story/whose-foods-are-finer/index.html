Whose foods are finer?
By Sarah Collins
5:15 a.m. May 27 2010

    D&D Finer Foods
    825 Noyes Street (847) 475-4344

    D&D Finer Foods. Photo by Abigail Dennis / North by Northwestern.

    In any reasonable competition, D&D will win. It carries more foods than Hanan’s and more of what Hanan’s does have. Plus, booze. And a restaurant next door serving breakfast, lunch and dinner diner staples like omelets, gyros and homemade Italian beef. D&D is a full-service grocery and deli. If you eat it, chances are you can get it here. And if you can’t, Kosta Douvekas, the owner’s son and heir to the store, will order it for you. “We’re the store that if nobody else carries it, we do,” he says. “I’m guessing about half of the things here are what people ask for, special order items.” Which is probably why they have an entire rack of wine and several freezer cases filled with micro-brew beer from Dogfish Head, Breckenridge and Bell’s Breweries, plus stock-in-trade like Goose Island and a few cases that look like they were bought just for the name (Moose Drool anyone?). They also have booze for the college kids — cases of Busch Light, Heineken mini-kegs, forties of Olde English, Mickey’s and Steel Reserve for $2.79 each, and bottles of Yellow Tail and Barefoot Bay for those drinkers looking to remember tomorrow. Just remember Douvekas’s warning: “I check IDs meticulously,” he says. “Hey, if you’re of age, I’m more than happy to sell it to you, but don’t try to come here otherwise.” So even if it works at Hundo, you’re better off sticking to the specialty soda aisle, sampling Berghoff and Goose Island’s root beers instead.

    Specialty: D&D shows its Mediterranean roots by selling several options of bulk olive oil and fresh Greek yogurt and tzatziki sauce. Douvekas also hosts wine tastings Thursday–Saturday from 3 p.m.–8 p.m. “I open up four bottles of wine and we could have a good time,” Douvekas says.

    Prices: $2.99 for a gallon of milk. $7.99 for Yellow Tail. $21.99 for a Heineken Keg.

    Hanan’s Finer Foods
    1942 Maple Avenue (847) 332-2402

    Hanan’s Finer Foods. Photo by Gus Wezerek / North by Northwestern.

    So sure, D&D might beat Hanan’s at the whole “selling food” business, but Hanan’s has its charms. It’s way closer for south campus students, and the prices are always competitive with CVS. Plus, nowhere else near campus is selling a single “I [HEART SYMBOL] Bingo” candle for $3.99. Hanan’s is consistent. It’s a place to go when you need to grab milk, eggs and cereal, or when there’s no toilet paper in the apartment. It stocks the basics well, with enough little tchotchkes scattered around the shop to make exploring worthwhile. They also excel in the coffee and delicious treats business. They brew fresh coffee, cappuccino and lattés, and they keep the beef jerky, cookies and potato chips within stumbling distance from the door. So whether it’s too late to make good culinary decisions or too early to see properly without caffeine, Hanan’s has it covered.

    Specialty: Hanan’s has a surprising selection of Indian food—frozen dinners, meal kits and ingredients—that no one else is carrying.

    Prices: $2.99 for a gallon of milk. $3.99 for various glass candle holders, like a high-heeled shoe and toilet.
