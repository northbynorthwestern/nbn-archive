Miss DM's trivia night? Play along at home!
By Hillary Proctor
6:03 p.m. Jan. 31 2007
    Dance Marathon held a Trivia Night at Prairie Moon on Jan. 31, and we brought some of the questions home so you could play along. How well do you know your middle school trivia? (Think 1995-2000.)

    Physical Education

    What member of the 1999 World Cup-champion U.S. women’s soccer team was known for taking off her jersey after scoring the game-winning goal?

    Between 1996 and 2000, the New York Yankees won the World Series four out of the five years. What team won the remaining year?

    In the 2000 Olympics, which Australian swimmer won five gold medals?

    In what year did Lance Armstrong win his first Tour de France?

    Language Arts

    Name the book described by the summary.

    “In a world with no poverty, no crime, no sickness and no unemployment, and where every family is happy, 12-year-old Jonas is chosen to be the community’s Receiver of Memories. Under the tutelage of the Elders, he discovers the disturbing truth about his utopian world and struggles against the weight of its hypocrisy.”

    “A boy named Milo visits the Island of Conclusions (you get there by jumping), learns about time from a ticking watchdog named Tock, and even embarks on a quest to rescue Rhyme and Reason!”

    “Everyone in town thinks Meg Murry is volatile and dull-witted, and that her younger brother, Charles Wallace, is dumb. People are also saying that their physicist father has run off and left their brilliant scientist mother. Spurred on by these rumors and an unearthly stranger, the tesseract-touting Mrs Whatsit, Meg and Charles Wallace and their new friend Calvin O’Keefe embark on a perilous quest through space to find their father. In doing so, they must travel behind the shadow of an evil power that is darkening the cosmos, one planet at a time.”

    “Nine-year-old Cassie Logan, growing up protected by her loving family, has never had reason to suspect that any white person could consider her inferior or wish her harm. But during the course of one devastating year when her community begins to be ripped apart by angry night riders threatening African Americans, she and her three brothers come to understand why the land they own means so much to their Papa.”

    “Billy and his precious coonhound pups romp relentlessly through the Ozarks, trying to “tree” the elusive raccoon. In time, the inseparable trio wins the coveted gold cup in the annual coon-hunt contest, captures the wily ghost coon, and bravely fights with a mountain lion.”

    If you did well, you might want to show up for DM’s next trivia night at the 1800 Club on Feb. 5. Unfortunately for some, you have to be 21 to go; Prairie Moon’s was the first (and only) 18+ trivia night.
