Fashion's five best blogs
By Abby Shure
5:04 p.m. Oct. 18 2009

    Whether you’re still waiting on Rachel Zoe to find you the perfect formal dress, reeling from Tim Gunn’s last critique on Project Runway, or scooping out style steals from Crossroads, you could still use some carefully crafted fashion advice. While it’s easy to fall into a fashion rut at college, it’s just as easy to get informed, or even inspired, from the comfort of your dorm room. Look no further for the best of fashion blogging: the sites that will keep you updated on who’s wearing what as well as what you should be wearing, with a hefty doses of vintage, celebrity and fabulous thrown in for good measure.

    Go Fug Yourself

    GFY will inspire you to:
    a. Check the mirror before you leave your dorm room. They catch everything.
    b. Leave the foolish gear at home, including anything see-through or anything without pants, including leggings.
    c. Learn as much as possible about semi-obscure ‘80s pop culture.

    Go Fug Yourself (GFY) takes a critical (and hilarious) look at celebrity fashion. Heather and Jessica, the “Fug Girls” who write the blog, feature fantastic dialogues from their ill-dressed celebrities, ask commenters for ways to improve an outfit and generally make a plea for celebrities to get their acts together and dress their beautiful selves the way they deserve. March at GFY Headquarters means “Fug Madness,” a tournament of 64 celebrities down to a final champion who dressed the most fugtastically (read: worst) in the past year. It’s fugging amazing.

    Because of Project Rungay, you will:
    a. Be on the lookout for a cool gay BFF who will call you “darling.”
    b. Find yourself falling in love with Michael Kors (who they call “The Dutchess”) and the ever-amazing Tim Gunn.
    c. Appreciate high fashion on an artistic level, but also be able to accept that it’s not all fabulous.

    Project Rungay

    Project Rungay: the name says it all. Two amazing gay men, fond of calling readers “darlings,” blog about fashion from every dress on every episode of Project Runway to deciding whether Shelly O.’s outfit is “in or out.” (They’re one of the many devoted Michelle Obama fashion bloggers, but they get a special mention for the sheer amount of love they throw at her with each post). They also cover the best of the four fashion weeks and give show recaps for Mad Men and Glee. Their casual and picture-filled blogging style is perfect for any reader and despite holding real jobs (I think), they manage to post a few times a day with tons of material.

    These two amazing gay guys are incomparable – they’re not as snarky as GFY, but they won’t hesitate to honestly deal with any garment thrown their way.

    The Cut, from New York magazine

    The Cut is key for any NU fashionista looking to keep up with the outside fashion “real world.” They feature daily posts with fashion, beauty and fragrance news, as well as information about online and in-store sample sales, though they’re mostly in New York. Their best work comes from both informational pieces about new fashion developments (they’re just as excited about the upcoming designer collaborations with Target as I am!) and their slideshows.

    If you read The Cut, you’ll know about:
    a. Michelle Obama’s favorite designers and how she stacks up against other leading ladies.
    b. Every conceivable piece of information about high fashion and couture.
    c. Fashion’s biggest players –from socialites to editors to celebrities.

    They recap The Hills each week, do a best/worst list of fashion magazines for each month and keep up-to-date with runway shows and designer news. They manage to keep a great sense of humor (how else do you “report” on Whitney Port’s new fashion line?) but also provide a ton of information for anyone who wants to know more about the fashion industry. Their daily reports about the nuts-and-bolts of the fashion industry are surprisingly interesting and definitely informative.

    This site is like the 20-something sister to the New York Time’s style section; it’s younger and fresher but still just as smart and well-dressed.

    After reading The Sartorialist, your friends will notice that:
    a. You start dressing fashionably to go out in the city in the hopes of ending up on a fashion blog.
    b. You’ve starting appreciating the small details in their clothes and are becoming braver with the way you mix patterns.
    c. You just keep begging to have your picture taken. All. The. Time.

    The Sartorialist

    The tagline of The Sartorialist says photographer Scott Schuman, the Sartorialist, was selected as one of Time Magazine’s Top 100 Design Influencers. It’s easy to see why – his photographs take fashion and turn it into art as he combs the world’s most fashionable cities day after day. He shoots major editors in haute couture and sky-high heels (like French Vogue’s Editor-in-chief Carine Roitfeld) as well as anonymous men in dashing suits and brightly-printed socks. While the blogging world has complained that he doesn’t feature “real” girls because his subjects are usually thin, his site houses a collection of beautiful pictures that display exemplary personal style. Despite Schuman’s newfound fame as a fashion photographer, his roots still lay in finding everyday, on-the-street beauty and his blog is the best way to stay updated. Imagine that he’s shooting as you walk up Sheridan to your next class in Tech.

    The Sartorialist has the pictures of outfits that you wish you wore when being followed around by an amazing photographer, replete with glow-y lighting and a pretty background. It’s a simple “best-of” for the world’s fashion capitals.



    Sea of Shoes

    Reasons I wish I were Jane Aldrige:
    a. Shoes.
    b. She did a collection for Urban Outfitters – she knows how to make her crazy style affordable.
    c. Her hot boyfriend is just as stylish as she is, and has his own fashion blog about menswear: It’s a match made in style heaven.

    Jane Aldridge, who has now been featured in Vogue and other major fashion publications, had her Sea of Shoes blog featured by Kanye West (and no, he wasn’t encroaching in some other blogger’s spotlight). She’s 18 years old, travels around the country and the world with her equally-fashionable mom and has the most obscenely ah-mazing shoe closet you’ve ever seen. Ever. Jane’s claim to fame is her quirky, avant-garde style, and it’s one that is best for inspiration, not emulation. She takes pictures of her family, her outings and most especially her outfits, explaining where each piece came from. She combines the most beautiful (and expensive and designer) shoes with outfits found in stores like Saks as well as thrift shops close to her Texas home.

    Jane is the girl you wish you had the courage to be when you pull on your J. Crew sweater and Uggs – start reading her blog and you’ll at least switch the Uggs for some beat-up cowboy boots, if not a pair of towering Prada booties in two-tone pink, in no time.
