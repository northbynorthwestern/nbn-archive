Tasty tailgate
By Myrtie Williams
2:45 p.m. Oct. 7 2010
    Sausage and peppers. Photo by Myrtie Williams / North by Northwestern.

    No matter where I go this time of year it’s guaranteed that someone will bring up football, the one sport (well one of many) that I just don’t understand. But every once in a while I’ll get up early on a Saturday morning, throw on my purple shirt and make my way up north to scream some good ole Northwestern cheers. And probably on my way I’ll stop by the SPAC parking lot to grab a bite to eat a one of a hundred tailgates out there. Since my game experience typically ends after the first quarter, my contribution is to the tailgate. Not that there is anything wrong about the idea of “kegs and eggs” but maybe it’s time to add something new to the spread.

    Tailgate sausages and peppers

    Spicy grilled sausages topped with stewed peppers and onions is an easy and tasty Italian classic that fits right in to any tailgate experience.

    What you’ll need:

    • 4 fresh Italian sausages, hot and sweet (about 1 pound)
    • 1 medium Spanish onion, halved and sliced into ½ inch strips
    • 2 medium green bell peppers, sliced into ½ inch strips
    • 1 medium yellow pepper, sliced into ½ inch strips
    • 4 crusty sandwich rolls (or substitute cooked white rice)
    • 3 tablespoons olive oil (or enough to coat a large saute pan)
    • ½ cup chicken broth
    • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
    • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
    • Salt and pepper
    • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)

    1. Coat the bottom of a large saute pan with the olive oil and set heat to medium. Cook the sausages in the pan, browning on all sides until they are just cooked through (about 12 minutes). Remove from the pan and set aside.
    2. In the same pan with the heat now set to medium-low, combine all of the peppers and onions and saute until golden and slightly softened (about 10 minutes).
    3. Add the garlic, tomato paste, red pepper flakes and cook together with the peppers and onions for about a minute before stirring in the chicken broth. Cover and reduce the heat to just barely a simmer. After the chicken broth is in you can add salt and pepper to taste, but not before since chicken broth has a tendency to be salty.
    4. While the peppers and onions simmer slice the cooked sausages on the diagonal into about two inch bite size pieces and combine with the pan mixture.
    5. Continue cooking until the sausages are heated through and the mixture has slightly reduced. Divide between sandwich rolls to serve.

      6. Serves four.

    Note: If you prefer cooking the sausages on a grill “hot dog style” then you can make the pepper and onion mixture ahead of time and serve it as a topping.
