Big Ten power rankings, Nov. 2
By Sammy Levitt
3:52 p.m. Nov. 1 2010

    1. Wisconsin:

    With Michigan State’s defeat, it was a toss up between the Buckeyes and Badgers for the top spot. Although they were idle during Hallo-weekend, the fact that Wisconsin still has only one loss and beat Ohio State gives them an edge. A key to Badger success down the stretch: Wisconsin does not play any more games against currently-ranked opponents for the rest of the season.

    2. Ohio State:

    Aside from their loss to an excellent Wisconsin team, the Buckeyes have dominated their opponents, including Minnesota, beating the Golden Gophers 52-10 on Saturday. Ohio State could be in the best position to win a conference that is wide open right now. Jim Tressel’s squad should beat Penn State and arch rival Michigan. The Buckeyes’ visit to Iowa City on Nov. 20 is looming as perhaps the biggest match-up in this race as the season winds down.

    3. Iowa:

    Here’s a statement: the Hawkeyes absolutely pounded the Spartans at Kinnick Stadium. Iowa also came ever-so-close to knocking off Wisconsin earlier this year. Quarterback Ricky Stanzi is enjoying another impressive season and the Hawkeyes may be the team that no one wants to face down the stretch this year. Their relentless performance on Saturday knocked Michigan State off its perch and opened up this conference to a lot of schools, including themselves.

    4. Michigan State:

    The Iowa Hawkeyes are a very good football team, but the Spartans didn’t foresee things going as bad as they did. Sparty not only tarnished their perfect record on Saturday, but the team also no longer controls its own destiny within the Big Ten. Michigan State’s loss opens the Big Ten championship race wide open, but luckily the Spartans have a fairly easy closing schedule in November.

    5. Illinois:

    The Fighting Illini continue to turn heads around the Big Ten and, with winnable games remaining on their schedule, could make a bowl appearance. Freshman quarterback Nate Scheelhaase turned in another solid performance against Purdue, going 16/20 on passing attempts for 195 yards. Ron Zook’s crew continues to play inspired football, finding themselves at fifth in the Big Ten heading into November, and could keep moving up.

    6. Northwestern:

    Northwestern took care of business against Indiana, and now the real test starts for Pat Fitzgerald and the ‘Cats. Northwestern will have to beat teams like Penn State, Iowa, Illinois and Wisconsin down the stretch to keep themselves afloat in this conference. The Wildcats are most likely going bowling for a third straight season, and they’ll need to continue the good vibes with some wins in November, beginning in Happy Valley on Saturday.

    7. Penn State:

    In another season, Penn State would be considerably lower on this list, seeing that they haven’t beat one formidable opponent thus far. However, with a win over the Michigan Wolverines and the distinct separation between the good and bad teams in this conference, the Nittany Lions find themselves in the middle of the pack. Joe Paterno’s team didn’t deserve that preseason top 25 ranking and may not deserve a bowl game unless they win down the stretch against good teams, starting with the Wildcats.

    8. Michigan:

    Rich Rod and the Wolverines needed to show progress over the course of this season, and they’re simply not doing the job. The Maize and Blue were unable to contain Penn State’s rookie quarterback Matt McGloin on Saturday night, and were never able to catch up after falling behind early. Rodriguez will need quarterback Denard Robinson and his squad to have an impressive finish to this season in order to keep his seat from burning this off-season.

    9. Purdue:

    After feeling the Buckeye wrath last week, the Boilermakers ran into another wall against Illinois. The Illini kept Purdue off the board for three quarters, as freshman starter Sean Robinson was unable to muster anything on offense. The Boilermakers have Wisconsin, Michigan and Michigan State slated as opponents over the next three weeks, so don’t expect much more excitement from Purdue this year.

    10. Indiana:

    Expect things to get worse in Bloomington, especially with the Hawkeyes coming in next weekend. The Hoosiers kept things close with Northwestern, who yet again failed to put away a sub-par conference foe. The bright spot in Indiana continues to be the play of QB Ben Chappell, who threw for 308 yards against the ‘Cats and will likely notch over 3,000 passing yards in his senior season.

    11. Minnesota:

    Is there anything good going on in Minnesota this fall? The Golden Gophers got blasted this week by Ohio State, 52-10, dropping them to 1-8 on the year and still winless in the Big Ten. Times won’t get any easier for Adam Weber & Co. as they are still scheduled to face Iowa and Michigan State.
