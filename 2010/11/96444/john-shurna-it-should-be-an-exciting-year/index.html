John Shurna: "It should be an exciting year"
By Sammy Levitt
5:59 p.m. Nov. 7 2010

    Photo provided by Northwestern Athletic Communications

    Coming off a breakout sophomore season, forward John Shurna is looking to help lead the Wildcats to their first ever NCAA tournament birth this year. Shurna was a key contributor in the 2009-2010 season, averaging 18.2 points per game and setting the Northwestern single-season record for points. His play garnered praise around college basketball, including Sporting News, which named Shurna the most improved player in the nation. The Illinois native was named a preseason candidate for the John R. Wooden award last month, and was a second-team All-Big Ten selection last season.

    Sporting News named you the most improved player in the nation last year. What changed for you on the court? Were there any teammates in particular that helped you?

    I give a lot of credit to my teammates for giving me confidence and putting me in positions where I could score points. I think Juice [Michael Thompson] helped me out a lot. He doesn’t really get the credit he deserves. He knows the ins and outs of this offense and he deserves a lot of credit.

    You were a consensus second-team All-Big Ten selection following last season. What do you need to improve on and accomplish this year in order to become a first-team selection?

    I’m not even thinking about that. I’m just trying to focus on helping the team win and trying to make the tournament.

    What do you think the chances are of this team making the tournament? What are your expectations for this team now?

    I think we have high expectations for ourselves. We have a lot of guys coming back, as well as the great freshmen coming in. JerShon Cobb has done really well these past couple of weeks so I think he’s going to help us out a lot. It should be an exciting year for us.

    A lot of people seem to think your season last year came out of thin air and it surprised many around the game. Was it a surprise for you at all?

    I don’t really think about it too much. I was trying to help the team and was trying to get better every day in practice. I give a lot credit to my teammates. It’s a team game and the only way we are going to do well is if we play as a team.

    As an upperclassman, and with the voices of guys like Jeremy Nash and Kevin Coble gone, what do you have to do as far as taking a greater leadership role?

    I think I can just try to continue to work hard day in and day out, set a good example, and try to help guys as they if they’re having trouble with anything. I’m trying to improve every day and help this team get better.
